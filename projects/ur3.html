Vision-Guided UR3 Robotic Drawing System

A ROS-based robotic drawing system that converts a captured selfie into vectorized contours and reproduces it on paper using a UR3 robot.

Problem

Transform a captured selfie into a drawable path and accurately reproduce it on paper.

My Role

Image processing, UR3 movement control, paper detection, and coordinate transformation.

Outcome

Built an end-to-end robotic drawing pipeline with smooth motion and accurate paper alignment.

System Workflow

The system captures a selfie image, removes the background, extracts facial contours, vectorizes the edges, optimizes the drawing path, maps the drawing area to the paper frame, and executes the final path using UR3 robotic control.

Image Processing Pipeline

Developed a complete image processing pipeline including background removal, grayscale conversion, facial landmark detection, edge extraction, contour processing, and vectorization. Three vectorization methods were explored: nearest neighbour, bilinear, and bicubic interpolation.

Paper Detection & Coordinate Mapping

Mounted an RGB-D camera on the end effector to detect paper corners in real time. Converted local camera coordinates into the UR3 global frame to ensure the final drawing was correctly aligned and scaled to the paper surface.

Robot Motion Control

Implemented smooth UR3 trajectory execution using inverse kinematics, MoveIt path planning, and joint trajectory monitoring. Pen length compensation was incorporated to maintain consistent contact quality during drawing.

Final Result

Successfully integrated perception, coordinate transformation, path planning, and robotic execution into a working physical system capable of drawing vectorized selfie portraits on paper.
