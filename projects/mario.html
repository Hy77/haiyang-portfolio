End-to-End Deep Reinforcement Learning for Super Mario

A CNN + DQN reinforcement learning system trained end-to-end to autonomously complete Super Mario Bros levels through feature learning, experience replay, and policy optimisation.

Problem

Train an agent to learn game-state representation and complete Mario levels through reinforcement learning.

My Role

Designed the CNN + DQN pipeline, environment wrappers, replay logic, training scripts, and evaluation experiments.

Outcome

Achieved consistent flag-reaching behaviour after approximately 60k training episodes by switching from separate CNN/DQN training to end-to-end joint optimisation.

Training Evolution

The first version trained the CNN feature extractor and DQN separately, which led to weak feature-policy alignment and inconsistent gameplay performance. In the second version, both networks were trained jointly as a single end-to-end model, allowing visual representation learning to directly optimise decision quality.

This redesign significantly improved policy stability and allowed the agent to complete the level much more consistently than the earlier training strategy.

And the final model was trained for approximately 60k episodes over around 50 hours on an NVIDIA RTX 3070, with the best checkpoint showing reliable level completion.

Policy Evaluation via Epsilon Decay Test

To further validate policy robustness, I conducted a 100-episode epsilon decay experiment. The first 50 episodes used full exploration (epsilon = 1.0), while the remaining 50 gradually reduced epsilon to 0.1 to shift toward exploitation.

The reward trend improved significantly as the agent transitioned from exploration to exploitation. Peaks near 3000 reward indicate successful flag completion and confirm that the learned policy generalised well beyond the original training run.

Key Findings

The most important lesson from this project was that feature learning and policy optimisation should be trained jointly in visually complex environments. Separating CNN pretraining from DQN learning introduced representation mismatch, while end-to-end optimisation produced a far more stable and successful policy.

It also strengthened my experience in PyTorch-based model development, GPU training workflows, custom environment abstraction, and experimental policy evaluation.
